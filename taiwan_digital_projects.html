What this is

Taiwan reports its development finance to the OECD as the provider "Chinese Taipei" — at the aggregate level since 2004 and, since 2023, partly at the project level. That activity-level reporting is what lets us see individual projects for the first time. We took Taiwan's entire reported CRS portfolio for 2023–2024 — 540 funding lines across 218 projects, about US$885M disbursed — and classified every line for digital content. This page is the result: every project with a genuine digital element, each with its authoritative TaiwanICDF page and corroborating sources. Every link was opened and confirmed to describe the project; none are guessed.

Almost all of Taiwan's aid is conventional — agriculture, livestock, scholarships, health equipment, loans. The digital slice is small but real, and it clusters in a few recognisable families: government information systems (health information systems in Paraguay, Somaliland and Honduras; e-government in Somaliland), public-safety and disaster tech (intelligent video analytics in St. Vincent; flood early-warning in Belize and Guatemala), digital health (Fiji's case-management app; medical imaging in Belize) and digital skills & smart agriculture (ICT training in St. Lucia; IoT smart farming in Thailand).

Read the numbers honestly — and mind the denominator. Digital is about 0.9% of Taiwan's ~$885M in reported ODA. But that total is misleading: about 85% of it is a handful of pooled "Global Development Program" budget lines, not discrete projects. Measured against Taiwan's identifiable bilateral project spending (~$80M), the digital share is closer to ~10%. We split the digital projects into two tiers — Tier 1, digital projects (digital tech is the core or a distinct workstream) and Tier 2, a digital thread (a conventional project with only a minor digital element) — so nothing is dressed up as more digital than it is. Figures are the 2023–2024 CRS slice; several projects run for more years and larger totals than shown.

How we identified these

We pulled the OECD CRS bulk file (via oda_reader), filtered to provider Chinese Taipei — 540 activity rows / 218 unique projects across 40 recipients for 2023–2024 — and classified every project for digital content against a four-way scheme: digital governance & public services, digital inclusion / human development, hard digital infrastructure, and non-digital. 15 projects carry a genuine digital element. We deliberately did not count projects where "technology" just means farm machinery or medical devices, or where a digital term is only a buzzword (e.g. conventional rice, livestock or "smart storage" projects).

We then researched each digital project on the open web, prioritising the TaiwanICDF site and the recipient government, and kept only links we could fetch and confirm. Durations, partners and outcome figures come from those confirmed pages. Note the dollar figures are the 2023–2024 CRS slice — several projects (e.g. Paraguay's health system, St. Vincent's video analytics) run longer and larger; TaiwanICDF does not publish per-project budgets. One project (Honduras) has no surviving ICDF page because Honduras switched diplomatic recognition to the PRC in March 2023; it is documented via the Honduran press.

Sources. Monetary data: OECD Creditor Reporting System, provider "Chinese Taipei", 2023–2024, via oda_reader. Project detail & links: TaiwanICDF project pages and annual reports (2024 Annual Report), plus recipient-government and partner sources cited per project. Provider context: OECD Development Co-operation Profiles — Chinese Taipei.
