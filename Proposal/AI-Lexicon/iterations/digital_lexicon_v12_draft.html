Methodology

Scope

This lexicon analyses 43 terms across 11 regulatory frameworks governing artificial intelligence in the EU and selected US states. Terms are organised into 8 concept families, each grounded in the EU AI Act as the primary benchmark framework.

The EU AI Act (AIA) serves as the analytical anchor because it provides the most comprehensive and systematic definitional framework for AI governance currently in force. US state regulations are assessed against AIA terminology to surface convergences, divergences, and gaps in regulatory language.

Concept families and clusters

The 8 concept families are derived from the EU–US Terminology and Taxonomy for AI (2024) and organised into 4 thematic clusters: Governance (actors and responsibilities), Technical system attributes (model and system types), Measurement (risk and modification thresholds), and Trustworthy (incident and safety reporting).

Jurisdictions

The lexicon covers: European Union (AI Act + GPAI Code of Practice), California (SB 53, SB 942, AB 2013), Colorado (SB 24-205), New York (A6453B / S8828), Texas (HB 149), and Utah (SB 149, SB 226). Not every jurisdiction has legislation covering every concept — absence is meaningful and reflected in the analysis.

Source selection

Only enacted legislation and official codes of practice are included as primary sources. Bills that have not yet passed are excluded. Where amendments exist (e.g., Colorado SB 25B-004 extending CAIA enforcement to June 2027), the most recent authoritative version is used.

CEPS interpretative notes

Each concept family includes interpretative notes authored by CEPS researchers. These notes synthesise the comparative analysis, identify regulatory divergences, and flag open questions for policymakers. They are not legal advice and do not represent the position of the European Union.

Update cadence

This lexicon reflects the state of legislation as of early 2026. AI regulatory frameworks are evolving rapidly — several US state bills have pending amendments, and the EU AI Act is entering its phased implementation period. Updates are planned as significant legislative changes occur.
