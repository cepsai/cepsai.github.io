# ICLR 2026 treemap — 2026-04-24 update

## Summary

Cross-checked our institution classification against **Microsoft Research's own
ICLR 2026 publications page** (106 papers listed). Recall was 90.4 % — we had
94 of 104 matchable papers tagged to Microsoft, 10 were missed because the LLM
extraction dropped secondary affiliations (e.g., The Markovian Thinker has
`Alessandro Sordoni¹,²` where ¹=Mila and ²=Microsoft Research, but Gemma captured
only the ¹Mila superscript).

A systematic audit of all 33,721 author-slots found **3,205 PDF-verified LLM
misses** (9.5 % of slots) where the author's OpenReview profile has an affiliation
the LLM dropped AND that affiliation is literally printed on the PDF. After
cleaning crosswalk duplicates and applying four logical checks, ~347 real
net-new misses remain — tractable enough for a final manual review.

## Pipeline changes

- **Crosswalk dedup pass** — automated detection of canonicalization duplicates
  via 4 heuristics (strict distinguishing-token signature, prefix-location suffix,
  reverse-comma, trailing `, <city>`, `.com` suffix, case-variants), with
  safeguards against single-token collisions (`massachusetts` → MIT vs UMass).
- **Missed-affiliation audit inspector** (`missed_aff_inspector.html`) — shows
  each author-slot where Gemma missed an OR-profile affiliation, with PDF
  page-1 snippet highlighted for eyeball verification.
- **Strict affiliation-zone verification** — re-checks the PDF for distinguishing
  tokens only *before* the ABSTRACT boundary, with email addresses stripped.
  Kills false-positives like `NVIDIA` matching inside `@nvidia.com` or paper body
  text ("on NVIDIA H100").
- **Logical coverage check** before flagging a miss as real:
  1. Acronym cross-match (`BIGAI` ↔ `Beijing Institute for General AI`,
     `ASML` ↔ `ASML Netherlands BV`, `KAIST` ↔ `Korea Advanced Institute…`)
  2. Equal distinguishing-token set (guarded against 1-token non-brand collisions)
  3. Bidirectional subset match (`Meta` ⊂ `Meta AI (FAIR)`, `ModelBest` ↔
     `ModelBest Inc`)
  4. Compound-split: if missed is `"A, B"` and either part canonicalizes to
     something already on the paper, it's covered
     (e.g. `"Mila, McGill University"` when paper has Mila)

## Merge additions applied

- Brand variant folds: `Facebook* → Meta`, `Google Research/LLC → Google`,
  `Microsoft Research/AI/Corporation → Microsoft`, `Huawei Technologies variants
  → Huawei`, `Alibaba Group variants → Alibaba Group`, `NVIDIA Research → NVIDIA`,
  `Tencent Inc./AI Lab/YouTu Lab variants → Tencent`, `AMD aliases →
  Advanced Micro Devices`, `Wechat AI → Tencent`.
- Canonicalization cleanup: `Ohio State University, Columbus → The Ohio State
  University`, `University of Texas at Austin → The University of Texas at
  Austin`, `Beijing University of Post and Telecommunication(s) → Beijing
  University of Posts and Telecommunications`, `Institute of automation, Chinese
  academy of science (lowercase) → Chinese Academy of Sciences`, `SalesForce.com
  → Salesforce`, `Ant Research → Ant Group`, `vivo AI Lab → vivo`.
- Mila absorbs University of Montreal + Université de Montréal variants.
- MBZUAI acronym + misspelling folded into canonical.

## Known bug fixed

`build_viz_v2.py` previously read `institution_crosswalk.json` which gets
mutated by every `apply_treemap_overrides.py` run. Subsequent build runs
picked up stale merges, occasionally mis-canonicalizing strings (e.g., HKUST
main mentions being absorbed into HKUST Guangzhou). Fixed by having
`build_viz_v2.load_crosswalk()` prefer the pristine `.bak_pre_overrides`
backup when it exists.
