## 1. Dataset definitions

- **v2** — per-author institutions extracted by **Gemma-4-31B** from the page-1 OCR of each paper, with a crosswalk + bias-review pipeline (acronym aliases, subsidiary pairs, manual per-author overrides) on top. Falls back to the raw OpenReview institution when the LLM is silent AND the OR string is ≥ 60 % present in page-1 text. Artifacts: `v5.csv`, `v15.json`.
- **v3** — per-author institutions are literally the `institutions` column from `iclr_2026_accepted.csv`, which is OpenReview's raw semicolon-separated author → institution map. No LLM, no PDF parse. Only clustering work: acronym+subsidiary equivalence via `build_iclr_merged.inst_match()` (Jaccard ≥ 0.5 on tokens, with user-approved aliases from `bias_overrides.json`). Artifacts: `v6.csv`, `v16.json`.

## 8. Tradeoffs and discussion

**Headline finding:** on large author-list papers v2 systematically **under-extracts** institutions. The LLM tends to collapse a 30-author multi-lab consortium paper down to 1-5 "hero" affiliations that appear on the front-page banner, missing the tail. OR, by contrast, has every author's self-registered affiliation in full. Example 14 above (TrustGen, 64 authors) is the most extreme: v2 returns 1 institution (Allen Institute for AI); v3 returns 37. Examples 7, 10, 13, 14 all show 1-institution v2 rows that v3 expands to 8-37 institutions.

This inverts the intuition that more post-processing = more accurate. The LLM's page-1 view is authoritative only for the few names that *render on page 1*; OR's 1:1 list is authoritative for the whole author list.

**When v2 is better**

- *Multi-affiliation authors that OR truncates to one entry.* When an author is at Shanghai AI Lab + SJTU, OR's 1:1 column only stores one of those. The LLM sees both on the PDF footer and extracts both. See the 278 v2-exclusive acronym/sub-entity clusters above (BIGAI, Tongyi Lab, Sony AI, Sakana AI, KAUST, StepFun, M-A-P, ...).
- *Industry subsidiaries.* The v2 crosswalk collapses DAMO Academy → Alibaba, Google Research → Google, AWS AI → Amazon. v3 shows raw OR strings that may split or mis-label these (e.g. "AWS" is OR-only, "Amazon" is both).
- *Oral-paper sub-lab granularity.* The page-1 banner on a flagship paper often credits a lab name (e.g. "Shanghai AI Lab") that OR profiles never record because no author has that lab as their primary employer.

**When v3 is better**

- *Long author lists (≥ 15 authors).* v2 drops co-authors the LLM can't attribute to a clear page-1 affiliation; v3 has 100 % coverage.
- *Completeness per author slot.* v3 fills 100 % of author slots (only 1 empty paper of 5,355). v2 has 30 empty papers and a long tail of empty per-author slots.
- *Reproducibility.* v3 is a 15-second pure-stdlib build from `accepted.csv`. v2 requires a GPU, a merged jsonl, a crosswalk run, and a bias review.
- *No LLM hallucinations.* v2 occasionally fabricates acronyms ("SUAT", "QHU", "BNBU") or mis-normalizes regional branches. v3 has only what OpenReview users typed.

**Known v3 artifact: HKUST → (Guangzhou) collapse.** OpenReview's dropdown appears to force Clear Water Bay and Guangzhou HKUST authors to the same "Hong Kong University of Science and Technology(Guangzhou)" label. In v3 this single cluster has 352 papers — top-2 overall — which over-represents the Guangzhou campus. v2 splits HKUST (126 papers) and its Guangzhou branch cleanly. We should live with this quirk in v3 or add a bias-override pair.

**Which should ship?**

**Default to v3** for the institution treemap. The long-author-list under-extraction in v2 is a larger distortion than v2's advantages (sub-lab granularity, multi-affiliation capture). For users who care specifically about *paper-level lab attribution* (e.g. tracking Shanghai AI Lab, DAMO Academy, Google Research Asia as distinct entities), surface v2 as an optional overlay.

**Recommended posture:** v3 is the new default; v2 stays live for comparison and is the right answer for questions about hero-lab attribution on PDF banners.
