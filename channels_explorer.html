A CRS channel of delivery is the type of organisation that actually carries out a project on the ground. The OECD records it for every reported activity, separating the donor that funds the work from the implementer that delivers it. Implementers fall into broad families: recipient governments, multilateral agencies, NGOs and civil society, universities and research bodies, private firms, and public-private networks.

Who executes matters because the channel shapes the aid modality. Funds routed through recipient government systems build state capacity and run on national budgets; NGOs and universities tend to deliver discrete projects; multilaterals pool resources at scale; private firms supply technology and build infrastructure.
