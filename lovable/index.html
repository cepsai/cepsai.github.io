EU in the AI supply chain

The sovereignty question isn't just "how many apps does Europe ship" — it's "where in the stack does Europe actually sit." Five layers from silicon to the end-user. The Index scores Europe at each layer so policy readers can see where the sovereignty argument is real and where it's still aspirational.

1. Compute / silicon — chips, accelerators, data centres. (Graphcore, SiPearl, Axelera, Kalray)
2. Foundation models — frontier LLMs and multi-modal base models. (Mistral, Aleph Alpha, Black Forest Labs, H, Poolside)
3. Model-infra / tooling — hosting, fine-tuning, evaluation, orchestration. (Hugging Face, Synthesia, ElevenLabs, Stability)
4. Vertical applications — AI-native products targeting end customers. (DeepL, Helsing, Wayve, Klarna-AI, Bending Spoons)
5. AI app-builders — platforms where non-technical founders ship apps. (Lovable, Bolt, v0, Replit, Cursor)

Regional share of each supply-chain layer synthetic · calibrated

Percent share of global activity by region, layer by layer. Numbers are analyst-consensus-style estimates, not measured — real methodology would combine funding data, active-user data, compute capacity, and market-share reports. The shape is what matters: Europe is weakest at compute and strongest at app-builders.
